Consulting Map Generator — FAQ

1. What does Consulting Map Generator do?

It creates interactive maps showing airports, routes, and range circles. You can customize styles, zoom, and export the map as PNG or copy it to your clipboard.

2. How do I add airports?

Enter one IATA code per line in the Airports section:

SJK
LHR
JFK

For range circles:

900nm@MIA
900km@MIA#ff0000

Manual entry with latitude/longitude:

XYZ 37.7749 -122.4194
3. How do I add routes?

Use hyphens to chain segments:

JFK-AMS
MIA-MAD-CDG
4. How do I group routes?

You can organize routes into groups using the keyword group followed by a name:

group Europe
MAD-FRA
LHR-CDG
CDG-AMS

Click Manage route groups to show/hide groups, change the color of routes and airports, adjust the line style, and set the route thickness for all routes within that group.

Note on priority: Individual route edits (like changing a single route's color or arc) will always override the group's settings. Group settings, in turn, override the global styles defined in the "Style" panel.

5. How do I group airports?

You can organize airports into groups using the keyword group followed by a name:

group Hubs
JFK
LHR
FRA

Click Manage airport groups to show/hide groups and collectively set the color/radius of the airport dots and the color/size/position of their labels.

Note on priority: Any style applied to an individual airport or label (by clicking on it) will always override the group's settings. Group settings, in turn, override the global styles.

6. How do I customize the map style?

Under Style, you can change colors for ocean, countries, borders, routes, airports, labels, and range circles. Adjust widths, sizes, and fonts for labels.

7. Can I adjust labels?
  • Toggle Show labels
  • Adjust orientation and distance
  • Click Reset label positions to restore defaults
  • Drag labels directly on the map
  • New: Click a label to open the per‑label editor (set color and font size for that label only)
8. How do I zoom or center the map?

Use the Zoom input for precise control. To center on an airport, enter its IATA code and click Center.

9. How do I render the map?

Click Render Map after entering airports and routes.

10. How do I export the map?

Click Download PNG or Copy PNG.

Important: The PNG size matches the map area on your screen. If you want the map to occupy half of a slide, reduce the map area to half of your screen size before exporting.

11. Can I edit individual routes?

Yes! Hover over a route and click it to change its color.

Globe only: You can also adjust the arc curvature and choose the route type (Arc or Great Circle Line) under the Options tab.

12. What if the map doesn’t load?

Ensure internet access. If on a corporate network, CDNs may be blocked. Check the Messages area for warnings.

13. Can I resize the control panel?

Drag the vertical resizer between the panel and map. Width is saved automatically.

14. How do I edit individual airport label visibility?

The Edit Airport Label Visibility feature lets you control which airport labels appear on the map without removing the airports themselves.

  • Click Edit Airport Label Visibility next to the Show labels checkbox in the Options panel.
  • A pop-up will show all currently plotted airports with their IATA codes and names.
  • Use the checkboxes to toggle each label on or off. Changes apply immediately if Show labels is enabled.
  • Use the search bar to filter airports by code or name.
  • Quick actions: Show all or Hide all labels at once.
  • Your manual label positions (dragged on the map) are preserved even when labels are hidden.

Tip: Hidden labels do not appear in exported PNGs or copied images.

15. What does the "Arc bending rule" drop‑down do?

The Arc bending rule controls the orientation of arcs when the Route type is set to Arc. It appears under the Options panel only when Route type = Arc.

  • Directional (east/west): arcs bend based on the segment's geographic direction. Eastbound segments bow up on the screen (toward the northern hemisphere), while westbound segments bow down. This is useful when drawing Hub-and-spoke routes.
  • Non‑directional (north-up): arcs always bow upward on the screen, regardless of east or west.
16. How do I hide the shared border between two adjacent countries?

You can visually merge two countries by hiding only the border they share.

  • Shift + right‑click a country to select it.
  • Shift + right‑click an adjacent country to toggle their shared border.
  • Press Esc to cancel a pending first selection.
17. How do I edit individual airports and labels?

Airports (dot color & radius): Click an airport dot to open its editor. Set a per‑airport color and radius.

Labels (color & font size): Click a label to open its editor. Set a per‑label text color and font size.

18. How do I edit individual country colors?

Click over a country and the color editor will appear on the screen. You can simply change its color by filling the RGB boxes or by dragging the selector to the desired color.

19. How do I add and edit Range Circles?

1. Radius around an Airport: Type the distance followed by the unit (nm or km), the @ symbol, and the airport's IATA code. Example: 900nm@MIA.

2. Custom Coordinates: Start with a name for your group, then list Latitude and Longitude pairs.

Edit: Click on any range circle border to adjust color, width, and line style individually.

20. What is the Range Circle Analyzer (OASYS)?

The Range Circle Analyzer (right panel, opened by the range icon on the right edge) lets you load aircraft performance data and plot range polygons on the globe.

  • Load data from the built-in database (DB_Range Circles.js) or upload an Excel file.
  • Filter by Departure Airport, Aircraft, Payload Definition, and Temperature Exceptions.
  • Adjust Passenger Weight — payload recalculates automatically.
  • Results are shown in a draggable panel on the map.
  • Use Show results on map to overlay range info on the globe.
  • Use Center on ranges to rotate the globe to face the range centroid.
21. How do I filter airports inside range polygons?

In the Range Circle Analyzer panel, enable Airport types in range to plot airports that fall inside the range polygons.

  • Select which types to show: Large, Medium, and/or Small airports.
  • Set Min/Max Daily Depts to filter by traffic volume.
  • Adjust Marker Size (Dot and Label) for all plotted airports at once.
  • Each airport marker is individually clickable — change its color and radius.
  • Labels are draggable and editable (color and font size).
  • The Center Marker (origin dot) can also have its Dot and Label size adjusted globally.
22. How do I select and move result panels on the map?

The range results and Mission Assumptions panels can be freely moved and resized on the map, just like PowerPoint objects.

  • Select: Click on a panel — a blue selection box with 8 handles appears.
  • Move: Drag the panel to reposition it anywhere on the map.
  • Resize: Drag any handle to resize the panel. The content stays vertically centered.
  • Multi‑select: Hold Ctrl and click to select multiple panels at once. All selected panels move/resize together.
  • Deselect: Click on empty space on the map.
  • Save/Load: Panel positions are saved in the JSON file and restored on load.
23. How does the globe view work?
  • Rotate: Click and drag on the globe to rotate it.
  • Pan (Shift+Drag): Hold Shift and drag to move the globe horizontally/vertically without rotating.
  • Zoom: Scroll wheel or use the Zoom input.
  • H. offset / V. offset / Rotation: Precise numeric controls for the exact globe position (saved in JSON).
  • Atmosphere: Toggle a subtle atmospheric glow effect around the globe edge.
  • Center on airport: Enter an IATA or ICAO code and click Center.
24. How do aspect ratio and Auto mode work?

The aspect ratio button (next to FAQ) cycles through: 16:9 → 4:3 → 1:1 → Auto.

  • 16:9, 4:3, 1:1: The map container maintains that ratio. Results appear on the left, globe shifts right.
  • Auto: The map fills the available space. When screen is narrow (vw/vh < 1.3), results go below the globe with vertical scrolling. Wide screens use the side layout.
25. What is dark mode and how do I enable it?

Click the 🌙 button next to FAQ to toggle dark mode. All panels, inputs, and the map adapt automatically. The preference is saved for the next session.

26. Can I use ICAO codes instead of IATA?

Yes. The tool now accepts both ICAO (4 letters) and IATA (3 letters) codes everywhere: airports input, routes, center map, and range circle definitions. The built-in database contains over 10k airports with ICAO+IATA mapping.

27. How do I save and load my work?

Click Save Map to download a JSON file with all settings — including airports, routes, range circles, styles, globe position, panel positions, RC filters, marker sizes, and theme. Click Load Map to restore everything exactly as it was.

28. Does the map auto-render?

Yes. Changes to the Airports, Routes, and Range Circles text areas trigger an automatic re-render. No need to click Render Map manually. You can still use the button if you want a forced refresh.
